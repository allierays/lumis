# LinkedIn Post

## Instructions

Write a LinkedIn post that sounds like a person talking, not a brand broadcasting. LinkedIn rewards saves, dwell time, and substantive comments. Write for those signals.

### Step 0: Gather Context

Ask the user:
1. **What do you want to say?** (the idea, story, lesson, or take)
2. **What format?** Text post, carousel outline, or video caption
3. **What do you want people to do after reading?** (comment, visit a link, save it, DM you)

If the user provides a story, article, video, or existing content, use that as source material instead of asking.

Read Voice.md from the vault if it exists. Match the creator's tone.

### Step 1: Write the Hook

The hook decides whether anyone reads the rest. 60-70% of potential readers are lost at the "see more" button.

Rules:
- Under 110 characters (safe for mobile truncation)
- One line, two max
- Blank line after the hook. Always.
- Must do one of three things: create curiosity, promise a specific outcome, or challenge a belief

Hook types that work:
- **Open loop**: "I got fired on a Tuesday. By Thursday, I had the best job of my career."
- **Bold claim**: "Most networking advice is backwards."
- **Specific number**: "5 things I learned from 200 failed cold emails."
- **Direct question**: "What's the one skill nobody taught you that changed everything?"
- **Reframe**: "What if the thing holding you back is the same thing that got you here?"

Hooks that don't work:
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "I'm excited to announce..."
- "Thoughts?" as the entire hook
- Anything that could be written by anyone about anything

Present 2-3 hook options to the user. Let them pick.

### Step 2: Write the Full Post

Pick the structure that fits the content:

**Story Post** (highest engagement for personal brand):
```
[Hook]

[Setup: 2-3 short lines of context]

[Tension: what went wrong or was hard]

[Turn: the moment things changed]

[Lesson: the specific takeaway]

[CTA: question inviting their story]
```

**Listicle / Value Post**:
```
[Hook: "X things I learned from..."]

[Brief context: 1-2 lines]

1. Point
2. Point
3. Point

[Closing insight]

[CTA: "What would you add?"]
```

**Contrarian Take**:
```
[Hook: bold statement]

[Why most people believe the conventional thing]

[What you've seen that contradicts it]

[Your alternative]

[CTA: "Am I wrong?"]
```

**Before/After**:
```
[Hook: the result]

[Where you started]

[What changed]

[The outcome with data]

[CTA]
```

Rules:
- 1,200-1,800 characters total (200-300 words)
- 1-2 line paragraphs max. Never a wall of text.
- Blank lines between every paragraph
- Conversational tone, not corporate
- 0-3 emojis max, only as visual anchors, never as decoration
- Links go in the first comment, never in the post body (60% reach penalty)
- End with a specific question or low-barrier CTA, not "Thoughts?"
- 3-5 hashtags at the end: 1 broad, 2 mid-tier, 1-2 niche

### Step 3: Apply Humanizer Rules

Before presenting the final post, check for and remove:
- AI vocabulary (delve, landscape, crucial, leverage, robust, innovative, tapestry, multifaceted, holistic, comprehensive, pivotal, groundbreaking, paradigm, foster, encompasses)
- Significance inflation ("stands as a testament to", "a powerful reminder that")
- Filler phrases ("It's worth noting", "Interestingly", "In today's X landscape")
- Em dash overuse. Use commas, colons, or periods.
- Rule of three ("informative, engaging, and transformative")
- Negative parallelisms ("It's not just X; it's Y")
- Sycophantic language
- Corporate polish / press release tone
- Generic conclusions ("The future of X is Y")

Read each paragraph out loud. If it sounds assembled, rewrite it.

### Step 4: Present and Revise

Show the post formatted exactly as it would appear on LinkedIn (with line breaks and spacing). Include:
- The post text
- First comment text (with any links)
- Suggested hashtags

Tell the user:
- Best posting times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM or 12-2 PM local time
- Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes
- If sharing a link, put it in the first comment

### Format-Specific Notes

**For Carousels:**
- 5-10 slides
- One key idea per slide
- Cover slide is the hook (treat it like a thumbnail)
- CTA on the final slide
- Write the companion post text using the same rules above

**For Video Captions:**
- Under 90 seconds of video performs best
- Captions are required (85% watch without sound)
- Hook in first 5 seconds of video
- Native upload only (never a YouTube link)
- The post text is shorter: hook + 2-3 lines of context + CTA

### What NOT to Do

- Never put links in the post body (first comment only)
- Never use engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree!")
- Never use more than 5 hashtags
- Never tag more than 2-3 people, and only if genuinely relevant
- Never post and ghost. Engage with commenters in the first hour.
- Never sell in every post. 80% value, 20% promotional.
- Never write a wall of text with no line breaks
- Never start with "I'm excited to share..." unless it's genuinely newsworthy
- Never use engagement pod tactics
- Never write something that could be written by anyone about anything. Be specific. Have opinions. Sound like you.
